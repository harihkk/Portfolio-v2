# Reference Parity Matrix

Maps every major section of the quality-benchmark reference experience to its "The Systems Journal" equivalent so we match the _experiential_ bar, taste, rhythm, confidence, without copying any of its copy, data, layout, or code.

## Purpose & Ground Rules

`shivanshgupta.com` is used here strictly as a **quality benchmark for craft and pacing**. We borrow the editorial _grammar_ (masthead conceit, hairline section rules, typographic hierarchy, dense-vs-exhale pacing, subtle scroll-reveal and count-up motion, near-monochrome palette) and reimplement it independently in our own stack and voice. We never reuse its markup, class strings, type-scale values, color tokens, reveal keyframes, component structure, copy, assets, numbers, or credentials. Source of the mapping below: the audit JSON at `docs/audit/raw-audit-2026-06-21.json` under `reference.sections`, `reference.parityMapping`, and `reference.cautions`.

The Systems Journal identity: warm paper palette (`#F2EEE5` paper, `#151412` ink, `#E34A2F` signal), Newsreader serif + Manrope sans + IBM Plex Mono, Next.js 16 App Router / React 19 / TypeScript strict / Tailwind v4 / GSAP. No photo, no video, no fabricated social/awards/publications.

## Rhythm & Pacing We Match (not copy)

The reference packs roughly fifteen sections yet never feels cluttered because each is gated by a full-width serif headline plus a thin border rule, giving the eye a hard reset between blocks. Pacing alternates deliberately, dense record sections interleaved with visual exhales. Hierarchy is almost entirely typographic: a single high-contrast serif at large `clamp()` sizes does the heavy lifting while body and metadata recede. Color is restrained and near-monochrome, which is what makes it read editorial rather than startup. Motion is subtle and single-purpose: count-up-on-scroll stats, gentle opacity/translate entrance reveals, reinforcing a "turning the page" metaphor rather than competing with content.

We reproduce this rhythm in our own palette and tokens (paper `#F2EEE5`, ink `#151412`, signal `#E34A2F`; Newsreader / Manrope / IBM Plex Mono; GSAP for motion). We adopt the masthead conceit only because we can back it with real editorial substance, a thin masthead over hype reads as costume.

## What We Deliberately Do NOT Replicate

Per the audit's `reference.cautions`, we reimplement everything independently and copy none of the following from `shivanshgupta.com`:

- **Code / markup.** No Tailwind class strings, no exact `clamp()` type-scale values, no `#1A1916` ink token, no cream/newsprint/border token names, no `opacity:0` + translateY/scale reveal keyframes, no component structure. Reimplement from scratch with our own tokens and stack.
- **Copy.** Every reference headline is theirs ("Building at the frontier of AI, mathematics & philosophy", "Four frontiers.", "What I've built and led.", "Things I've coded.", "Published work.", "Honors & awards.", "Moments.", "Essays & newsletter.", "Let's work together.", "Letters by Shivansh"). We write entirely new copy in our own voice.
- **Photos & video.** No portraits or photographs (the hero is an interactive systems plate, not a photo), no Instagram/moments imagery, no logos, no embedded video.
- **Social grids as social proof.** No social-media grids or handles; engineering social proof (packages, releases, CI, artifacts) replaces them entirely.
- **Fake awards / publications.** No fabricated awards (their "Young Changemaker 2025" is theirs and unverified for us). No self-authored publications attributed to Hari, the Research Desk surfaces EXTERNAL papers only, with ownership kept explicit to avoid implying false authorship.
- **Their data & credentials.** Never reuse their numbers (250+, 284+, 100+, 15+, 300+, 7+) or credentials (Team India, Eskom ISF, the named papers/projects). Every metric must be Hari's own and verifiable.
- **Costume risk.** The masthead/Vol./No./Est. framing is a genre convention we adopt, but only matched with real editorial substance.
- **Old-portfolio carryovers.** The dark "chaos-mode" theme and physics/sandbox canvas from the old site are intentionally not carried into v2.
